**Title:** Notion vs Monday — are they even competing for the same users at this point?

**Body:**
I've seen this debate come up a lot and I think people are comparing them wrong. Notion is a knowledge base that can sort of do project management. Monday is a project tracker that can sort of hold documentation. They're not really the same product. If your team lives in docs and wikis, Notion wins. If you're managing deadlines and assignments across people, Monday is cleaner. This comparison lays out the actual differences pretty well if you're trying to make the call: https://topstacktools.com/blog/notion-vs-monday-2026

## QUORA ANSWER 1
**Answer question:** "What is the best free CRM for a small business in 2026?"
**Link:** https://topstacktools.com/blog/best-free-crm-2026

The honest answer is: it depends on how many contacts you're managing and whether you need email sequences built in. For most solo operators or teams under 5 people, HubSpot Free or Zoho Free cover the basics without costing anything. The catch with HubSpot is that automation kicks in at the paid tier, so if you want workflows, you'll hit a wall quickly. Zoho tends to be more generous with features but has a steeper learning curve upfront. This review goes deeper into the actual limits of each free plan — worth a read before you commit: https://topstacktools.com/blog/best-free-crm-2026

## QUORA ANSWER 2
**Answer question:** "Is Systeme.io worth it? How does it compare to ClickFunnels or Kartra?"
**Link:** https://topstacktools.com/blog/systeme-io-review-2026

Systeme.io is worth serious consideration if you're an early-stage creator or solopreneur — the free plan is genuinely functional, not a stripped demo. You get funnels, email automation, and course hosting all in one place at a price point that's hard to argue with. ClickFunnels has better polish and a larger community, but you're paying a significant premium for that. Kartra sits somewhere in the middle but often feels overcomplicated for what most people actually need. If you want a side-by-side of what each plan actually includes before you pay for anything, this review breaks it down clearly: https://topstacktools.com/blog/systeme-io-review-2026
